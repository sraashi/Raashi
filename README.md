I'm a mechanistic drug discovery scientist specializing in the biophysical and biochemical characterization of small molecules and novel modalities — molecular glues, degraders, and protein–protein interaction disruptors. My background is in pre-steady-state enzymology and biophysical platform work (SPR, BLI, pre-steady-state kinetics), and I build Python tooling to connect in vitro binding and pharmacology data to mechanism.
PhD in biophysics (UW–Madison, Record lab); industry experience as a Senior Scientist in Quantitative Biology.
What's in these repos
These projects show how I bring quantitative and computational methods to drug discovery problems using real, public data:

Perturbation activity scoring in RxRx3-core — within-plate cosine-distance scoring of Cell Painting morphological embeddings, with control-based QC validation. Recovers known bioactive compounds and essential-gene knockouts from 222K wells of public Recursion data.
Michaelis–Menten kinetics in PySB — rule-based systems-biology modeling of enzyme kinetics, an extension of my mechanistic enzymology background into simulation.
4PL dose–response fitting in Python — moving curve fitting from GraphPad Prism into reproducible NumPy/SciPy code.
Compound potency prediction (capstone) — QSAR-style regression/classification on the BACE dataset.
